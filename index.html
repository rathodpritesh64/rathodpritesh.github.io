My Journey

From Analyst to Product Owner

I started in Business Analysis — digging into workflows, talking to stakeholders, and writing requirements that actually made sense to engineers. It taught me to ask "why" before "what", and to treat every process as a product waiting to be improved.

At B&Q Ireland, I stepped into a product ownership role and realised the shift wasn't about job titles — it was about accountability. I owned the backlog, ran experiments, tracked adoption, and saw the direct line between a prioritization call and 22% productivity gains for 5,000+ people.

Today at VisionWaves, I work on AI-driven feedback intelligence — defining NLP feature strategy, aligning with business goals, and shipping roadmap items that cut manual analysis by 30%. The craft is the same: understand the problem deeply, sequence the solution wisely, and measure everything.
